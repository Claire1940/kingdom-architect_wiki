<Section icon={Swords} title="Economic Buildings and Income Farming">

To climb the ranks of the **kingdom architect buildings tier list**, you must master the art of the reinvestment cycle. Every building has a cost and a payout frequency. In the current 2026 meta, maximizing your "active play" time involves a specific sequence of construction.

| Building Name | Category | Main Use | Placement Tip |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Establish the Village Core">
    Start your kingdom by placing 4-6 Houses and 2 Markets. This creates enough passive income to keep you building without waiting for long periods.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Collection Routine">
    Move through your village in a circular pattern to collect payouts. Doing this regularly ensures that building timers are always resetting.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Reinvesting for Upgrades">
    Before buying new land, check the "Upgrades" menu. Improving the efficiency of existing buildings is often cheaper than buying the land required for new ones.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Optimizing Offline Earnings">
    Before logging out, spend all remaining gold on the highest-tier buildings you have unlocked. Your kingdom earns money based on the total value of placed structures while you are away.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Budget Management">
Avoid spending more than 10% of your total gold on "Decorations" or "Trees" until you have unlocked at least three land expansions. Decorative items provide zero return on investment.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Infrastructure and Castle Expansion">

As your village grows into a kingdom, the B-Tier structures—Towers and Walls—become vital. While they don't generate gold, they provide the "Kingdom Architect" experience by transforming a cluster of houses into a fortified city. 

| Feature | Walls | Towers |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Primary Goal** | Boundary definition | Verticality and focal points |
| **Best Placement** | Outer perimeter | Corners and gate entrances |
| **Cost Efficiency** | Medium | High (per unit impact) |
| **Layout Role** | Separates districts | Emphasizes major intersections |

When transitioning to the Castle District stage, use walls to create "internal" courtyards. This allows you to separate your high-income Market districts from your residential Village zones, creating a more realistic and organized medieval layout.

<Callout type="info" title="Planning Tip">
Use "Terrain" and "Water" tools sparingly near your walls. It is much easier to build a straight defensive line on flat ground than to navigate complex elevation changes.
</Callout>

</Section>
