<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Road Layout and Progression">

Building a sprawling castle city requires a methodical approach. You cannot build a grand fortress without first establishing a humble, connected village. Follow this progression path to transition your settlement smoothly.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Establish the Starter Village Spine">
    Begin on your starter plot by placing a single, straight main road. Group your initial houses and markets along this spine. Keep this zone compact so that you do not waste valuable starting space on long, empty paths.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Extend the Network to New Land">
    When you purchase your first land expansion, immediately extend your primary road into the new territory. Do not place any buildings in the new plot until this road connection is clearly laid out.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Design the Commercial Market Street">
    Develop a dedicated shopping street (such as a themed Fruit Street) utilizing parallel roads. Place vendor and market buildings directly facing the street, and decorate the roadsides with small trees and lights to make the district stand out.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Fortify the Castle Core">
    Once you unlock towers and walls, build a dedicated castle district. Connect the entrance gate to your main road network, and use wide plazas or formal paved paths to lead players into the inner courtyard.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid Dead Ends">
Avoid creating dead-end roads in your core commercial zones. Dead ends restrict your visual layout options and make it difficult to integrate future building unlocks into your existing town center.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Best Practices for Landscaping and Decorations">

Once your **kingdom architect roads and paths** are established, you can begin beautifying your kingdom. Landscaping elements like gardens, trees, custom terrain, and water features should always be placed *after* your infrastructure is complete.

| Landscaping Element | Recommended Use | Design Tip |
|--- |--- |--- |
| **Gardens** | Fill awkward empty spaces between houses | Use symmetrical paths to frame a central fountain |
| **Water Features** | Create natural barriers or moats | Run a scenic road directly parallel to the shoreline |
| **Trees & Forests** | Soften the edges of your stone castle walls | Cluster trees in groups of three for a natural look |
| **Terrain Shifting** | Elevate your castle core above the village | Build winding stairs or sloped paths up the hills |

<Callout type="success" title="Decoration Rule">
Never let decorations block your expansion paths. Keep large water bodies, heavy forests, and major terrain shifts near the outer edges of your claimed land until your final city footprint is fully established.
</Callout>
