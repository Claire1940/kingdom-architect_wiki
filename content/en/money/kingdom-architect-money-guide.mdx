<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Money Farming Route">

To reach the "Large Kingdom" status in 2026, players should follow a structured progression route. This minimizes wasted resources and ensures you are always moving toward the next major land expansion.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Establish the Village Base">
    Start by filling your initial plot with as many Houses as possible. Don't worry about perfect aesthetics yet; focus on creating a dense cluster of income-generating units. Use basic Roads to connect them to the entrance.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Payout Collection Loop">
    Develop a habit of "sweeping" your kingdom. Move in a circular pattern to collect payouts. As soon as a building shows a reward icon, grab it. This manual activity is the fastest way to generate liquid cash in the early game.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Strategic Reinvestment">
    Use your first 10,000 to 50,000 coins to unlock the "Market" category. Markets generally have a higher payout per collection than basic Houses. Replace your least efficient paths with Market stalls to increase your revenue density.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="First Major Land Expansion">
    Save your funds specifically for the first land plot expansion. Buying land is expensive, but it is the only way to unlock higher-tier building slots. Once the new land is purchased, extend your main road immediately.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Transition to Castle City">
    Once you have 3-4 plots of land, begin placing Walls and Towers. At this stage, your income from the first two plots should be enough to fund the expensive decorative and defensive structures of a true Castle City.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid Over-Decoration">
It is tempting to spend your first 5,000 coins on Trees and Gardens. **Do not do this.** Every coin spent on decoration in the first hour is a coin that isn't earning you interest. Wait until you have at least two plots dedicated entirely to income.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Clock} title="Maximizing Offline Earnings and Growth">

One of the most powerful features in Kingdom Architect is the ability to earn while you are away. However, many players return to find their earnings are lower than expected because they didn't "prime" their kingdom for offline growth.

| Action | Impact on Offline Growth | Preparation Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Fill All Slots** | Maximizes potential revenue per hour. | Mandatory |
| **Upgrade Income** | Increases the base value of offline rewards. | High Priority |
| **Clear Payouts** | Ensures the "buffer" starts at zero. | Essential |
| **Plan Next Build** | Allows for immediate spending upon return. | Recommended |

Offline growth follows a specific logic: the game calculates the average income of your placed structures and applies a multiplier based on your time away. To get the most out of this system, you should focus on "Kingdom Upgrades" that specifically boost passive collection rates.

<Callout type="info" title="Offline Logic">
Your kingdom continues to progress even when the Roblox client is closed. When you log back in, you will receive a summary of your accumulated wealth. Use this "bulk cash" to buy your most expensive land expansions or high-tier castle unlocks.
</Callout>

The "Fruit Street" concept is a popular layout strategy where one entire street is dedicated to commercial vendors. This creates a visually stunning "Old Town" feel while concentrating your highest-earning buildings in one easy-to-manage area.

| Expansion Stage | Main Objective | Recommended Spend |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Starter Village** | Establish Income | Houses, Basic Markets |
| **Commercial Street** | Scale Revenue | Advanced Markets, Main Road |
| **Castle District** | Fortification | Walls, Towers, Gates |
| **Large Kingdom** | Final Detailing | Terrain, Water, Gardens |

<Callout type="tip" title="Layout Secret">
Leave at least a 2-unit gap between your buildings and the edge of a plot. This allows you to place Roads and Walls that seamlessly connect to the next piece of land you buy, preventing a "gridlocked" city layout.
