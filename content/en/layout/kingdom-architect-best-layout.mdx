While it is tempting to focus on the "Castle" elements immediately, your primary goal in the early game is capital accumulation.

Houses provide the most consistent footprint for building a dense village. By grouping houses in 2x2 or 3x3 blocks with small paths between them, you can maximize the number of payout points within a small area. Markets, on the other hand, should be placed along your busiest roads to simulate a realistic medieval trade center.

<Section icon={Construction} title="Step-by-Step Layout Development">

The transition from a small settlement to a grand kingdom should be handled in phases. Following a structured development route prevents you from having to demolish and rebuild large sections of your city later on.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="The Starter Village Anchor">
    Begin by placing a single straight road from the center of your plot toward one edge. Line this road with **Houses** and at least two **Markets**. This creates your initial income base and establishes the direction of your kingdom's growth.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Commercial Street Expansion">
    As you earn money, purchase your first land expansion in the direction your main road is facing. Extend the road and dedicate this new area to a "Fruit Street" or market district. Use **Decorations** like crates or small trees to give this area a distinct commercial feel.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Establishing the Castle Core">
    Once your income is stable, identify a large plot of land to serve as the castle district. Use **Walls** to create a perimeter and **Towers** at the corners. This area should be the most organized part of your layout, featuring symmetrical paths and central gardens.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Natural Landscaping and Finishing">
    With the main structures in place, use **Terrain**, **Water**, and **Trees** to fill the "dead space" between districts. Create a river that flows alongside your castle or a dense forest that buffers your residential area from the edge of the map.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Expansion Stage | Goal | Key Structure | Money Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Stage 1** | Stability | Starter Houses | **100% Income** |
| **Stage 2** | Growth | Main Road | **70% Income / 30% Roads** |
| **Stage 3** | Definition | Castle Walls | **50% Income / 50% Walls** |
| **Stage 4** | Perfection | Gardens/Water | **20% Income / 80% Decor** |

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid Crowding">
Do not pack buildings too tightly. Leaving a 1-2 stud gap between structures allows you to add small details like fences or flowers later, which significantly improves the final look of your layout.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Layout Archetypes for Different Playstyles">

Depending on your goals—whether you want to reach the max level as fast as possible or build the most beautiful kingdom on Roblox—different layouts will serve you better. The **kingdom architect best layout** is ultimately the one that fits your personal objectives.

### The Efficiency Grid
This layout is designed for players who want to maximize every square inch of land for income. It uses a grid-based road system with minimal decoration.
- **Pros**: Highest possible income; easy to navigate.
- **Cons**: Can look "boxy" or repetitive.

### The Natural Village
This style ignores strict grids in favor of curved roads and organic building placement. It utilizes trees and terrain to make the village look like it grew naturally over time.
- **Pros**: Highly aesthetic; unique look.
- **Cons**: Harder to plan; less space-efficient for buildings.

### The Fortified Citadel
A layout centered entirely around a massive central castle. All roads lead to the castle gates, and the residential areas are tucked safely behind secondary walls.
- **Pros**: Impressive scale; clear theme.
- **Cons**: Very expensive; requires significant land expansion.

| Archetype | Complexity | Income Speed | Visual Appeal |

| **Efficiency Grid** | Low | **Excellent** | Moderate |
| **Natural Village** | **High** | Good | **Excellent** |
| **Fortified Citadel** | Moderate | Moderate | **Excellent** |
