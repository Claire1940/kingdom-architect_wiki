<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Layout Construction Guide">

Following a structured approach to your **kingdom architect village layout** ensures that you don't run out of space or money. Use the following steps to transition from a humble settlement to a sprawling medieval capital.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Establish the Central Village">
    Begin in your starter plot by placing a compact cluster of houses and markets. Keep them close together but organized. This central hub will be your primary source of income for the first few hours of gameplay.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Define the Main Thoroughfare">
    As soon as you unlock Roads, build a straight path from the center of your starter plot toward the edge of the next available land purchase. This "Main Street" will act as the anchor for all future districts.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Expand into Commercial Districts">
    Purchase your first expansion plot and dedicate it to a specialized market street. Inspired by the popular "Fruit Street" builds, align your markets along the road with small decorative paths leading to residential pockets.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Fortify the Kingdom">
    Once you have a 2x2 or 3x3 grid of plots, begin placing walls and towers around the perimeter. This transforms your "village" into a "fortified city" and provides the structure needed for a central castle keep in the middle plots.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Success Metric">
A successful layout should allow you to walk from one end of the kingdom to the other via roads without hitting a dead end or a misplaced building.
</Callout>

</Section>
