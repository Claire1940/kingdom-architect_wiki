<Section icon={Shield} title="Economic Infrastructure and Tax Efficiency">

The heart of your kingdom's growth lies in its economy. In Kingdom Architect, managing your gold flow is a balancing act between building new structures and upgrading your existing infrastructure. The **kingdom architect building order** dictates that you should prioritize income-generating buildings over decorative ones until you reach a stable hourly profit.

Manual tax collection is the primary bottleneck for new architects. Each Peasant Hut has a storage cap, meaning if you do not collect the coins, the building stops generating profit. This makes the "Auto Collect" game pass or the in-game Tax Collector NPC essential milestones for any serious player.

| Economy Milestone | Target Cost | Benefit | Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **First 5 Huts** | 500 Coins | Stable early income | High |
| **Apple Stall** | 200 Coins | Unlocks food quests | High |
| **Tax Collector NPC** | 3,000 Coins | Automates gold collection | Critical |
| **Land Expansion** | 5,000+ Coins | Increases building area | Medium |

<Callout type="warning" title="Warning">
Avoid spending all your gold on land expansion too early. If you have empty land but no money to build houses, your progression will grind to a halt.
</Callout>

Beyond the basic huts, the Apple Stall serves as your first commercial venture. This building is required for several early-game quests and provides a different revenue stream that isn't strictly tied to the number of peasants in your kingdom.

<Section icon={Target} title="Optimal Building Order Progression">

To maximize your efficiency, follow this specific **kingdom architect building order** designed for the 2026 meta. This sequence ensures you never run out of gold or food while meeting the requirements for all early-game quests.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="The Tutorial Core">
    Construct your first Peasant Hut and Granary immediately. Collect the initial taxes to complete the tutorial and unlock the full build menu.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Residential Scaling">
    Build an additional 3 Peasant Huts. This brings your total to 4, creating a sufficient tax base to fund your next commercial building.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Commercial Integration">
    Purchase the Apple Stall for 200 coins. This satisfies the quest requirements and begins the transition from a purely residential village to a functional town.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="The Seven House Push">
    Focus exclusively on reaching 7 total houses. This is a major quest milestone that rewards a significant amount of gold, which should be reinvested into the Tax Collector.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Automation and Expansion">
    Save 3,000 coins to hire your first Tax Collector. Once collection is automated, you can focus on land expansion and higher-tier Cottages.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Expert Insight">
If you find the tax collection speed too slow, check the shop for the "2x Money" or "2x Timer Speed" boosts. While these cost Robux, they drastically alter the optimal building order by allowing you to skip the Peasant Hut spam phase.
</Callout>

</Section>
