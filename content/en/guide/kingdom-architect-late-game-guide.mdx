<Section icon={Target} title="Advanced Land Expansion and District Planning">

Expanding your kingdom is not just about buying more space; it is about assigning a purpose to every new plot. In the late game, a disorganized layout will lead to "dead zones" where income is low and navigation is difficult. By categorizing your land into specific districts, you can optimize both the visual appeal and the economic output of your kingdom.

When planning your expansion, consider the cost-to-benefit ratio of each new land purchase. Late-game plots are significantly more expensive, making it vital to have a pre-existing income base that can cover the costs.

| District Type | Best Buildings | Strategy | Space Requirement |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Commercial** | Markets, Stalls | Place along main roads | Medium |
| **Residential** | Houses, Manors | Group in quiet corners | Large |
| **Fortified** | Walls, Towers | Use as outer boundaries | Narrow/Long |
| **Natural** | Trees, Water | Fill gaps between districts | Variable |

<Callout type="warning" title="Expansion Warning">
Do not buy new land until you have fully utilized at least 80% of your current plots. Expanding too quickly can lead to a "hollow" kingdom that looks empty and generates insufficient income for its size.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Late Game Building Strategy and Layouts">

In the late game of Kingdom Architect, building placement becomes a puzzle. You must balance the need for income-generating structures with the desire for a realistic medieval aesthetic. The "Fruit Street" concept—a specialized commercial road—is an excellent example of how to combine these two goals.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Establish the Main Arterial Road">
    Create a primary road that spans across multiple plots. This will serve as the spine of your kingdom, connecting the starter village to the new castle districts.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Zone Your Commercial Spine">
    Place Markets and commercial buildings along the main road. This creates a high-traffic "downtown" area that makes collecting payouts much faster.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Enclose the Castle District">
    Once your income is stable, use Walls and Towers to encircle a high-value area. This provides the "Late Game" look and signifies your progression to a full kingdom.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Detail with Terrain and Water">
    Use the terrain and water tools to create natural barriers or scenic lakes. This should be the final step, as these elements do not generate income but take up valuable space.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Building Category | Late Game Role | Income Potential | Unlock Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Houses** | Population/Progression | Moderate | High |
| **Markets** | Primary Revenue | High | Essential |
| **Towers** | Visual Landmark | None | Medium |
| **Gardens** | Aesthetic Filler | None | Low |

<Callout type="success" title="Efficiency Tip">
Group your markets together in a single "Market Square." This allows you to click and collect all payouts in a matter of seconds, rather than running across the entire map.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Maximizing Offline Earnings and Growth">

One of the most powerful features in Kingdom Architect is the ability to progress while you are away. For late-game players, offline earnings are essential for funding the massive costs associated with final-tier land expansion and castle upgrades.

Offline growth scales with the number and quality of buildings you have placed. Therefore, a kingdom packed with high-level houses and markets will yield significantly more gold than a sparsely decorated one.

| Time Offline | Estimated Reward | Best Use of Funds |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1-2 Hours** | Small Boost | Minor decorations/Paths |
| **4-8 Hours** | Moderate Gain | New building unlocks |
| **12-24 Hours** | Large Payout | Land expansion/Castle walls |
| **48+ Hours** | Massive Surge | Multiple plot purchases |

<Callout type="info" title="System Note">
Offline earnings are calculated based on your total kingdom value and building count. The more you build before logging off, the more you earn while you sleep.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Final Kingdom Detailing and Aesthetics">

Once the economic engine of your empire is running smoothly, you can focus on the "Architect" part of Kingdom Architect. This involves using non-functional items like trees, terrain, and water to turn a grid of buildings into a living world. 

Late-game detailing focuses on:
- **Waterfront Quarters**: Using the water tool to create docks or riverside housing.
- **Forest Edges**: Surrounding your kingdom with dense tree clusters to define the map's edge.
- **Formal Gardens**: Creating symmetrical patterns with paths and flowers inside the castle walls.

| Landscaping Tool | Recommended Use | Visual Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Terrain** | Creating hills for towers | High |
| **Water** | Moats or scenic ponds | High |
| **Trees** | Filling empty plot edges | Medium |
| **Decorations** | Adding detail to streets | Low |

<Callout type="tip" title="Final Advice">
Don't be afraid to delete and replace early-game structures. As you unlock better buildings, your starter village may look outdated. Replacing old houses with newer, late-game variants will keep your kingdom looking consistent.
</Callout>
