<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Beginner Progression: Building Your First Village">

Starting your journey in Kingdom Architect requires a strategic approach to resource management. You begin with a small plot of land and a limited budget. The goal is to establish a functional settlement that generates enough money to fund your transition into a sprawling castle city. Because there are no **kingdom architect codes** to provide an initial boost, your early-game placement of houses and markets is critical for long-term success.

<Callout type="tip" title="Layout Planning">
Always leave at least one side of your village open. This ensures that when you purchase adjacent land, you can extend your road network without having to demolish existing structures.
</Callout>

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Establish Residential Zones">
    Place your first houses in a compact cluster. Residential buildings are the backbone of your kingdom's progression and provide the population base needed for growth.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Develop Commercial Districts">
    Build markets near your houses. Markets serve as your primary commercial hubs and significantly contribute to the total money collected during payout cycles.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Connect with Infrastructure">
    Use the Roads and Paths tool to link your buildings. A well-organized road network not only improves the aesthetic of your village but also defines the boundaries for future districts.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Collect and Reinvest">
    Regularly check your buildings for available payouts. Instead of spending early profits on decorations like gardens or trees, reinvest that money into more income-generating structures.
  </Step>
</Steps>

</Section>
