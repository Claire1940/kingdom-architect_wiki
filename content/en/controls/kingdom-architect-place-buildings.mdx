<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Building Placement Strategy">

To progress efficiently from a small settlement to a massive fortified city, you must follow a structured development plan. Placing structures randomly will lead to layout bottlenecks and slow down your overall money-farming speed.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Establish the Village Core">
    Begin on your starter plot by placing basic residential houses. Group these houses closely along a single straight road to maximize space efficiency while keeping them accessible for quick payout collection.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Construct the Commercial District">
    Unlock and place Markets near your village entrance or along a designated commercial street. Keeping your income-producing buildings centralized makes it much faster to run through and collect your building payouts.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Extend the Infrastructure">
    As you accumulate gold, purchase your first land expansion. Immediately extend your main road network into the new plot before placing any new buildings, ensuring the two districts are cleanly integrated.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Add Defensive Boundaries">
    Once your main village and commercial zones are stable, outline your kingdom using Towers and Walls. Position towers at key corners and intersection points to give your settlement a distinct medieval castle aesthetic.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Apply Natural Landscaping">
    Finish your layout by shaping the surrounding environment. Place trees, adjust terrain elevation, add water features like rivers or ponds, and place gardens in the remaining empty spaces between your major districts.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid Early Landscaping">
Do not spend your early game gold on trees, water features, or gardens. These decorative items do not generate income and will slow down your ability to purchase more land and unlock advanced building tiers.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Layout Concepts for Advanced Kingdoms">

Once you have unlocked multiple land plots, you can begin designing specialized districts. A well-planned kingdom uses distinct zones to separate residential, commercial, and defensive areas.

### The Commercial Market Street
This layout is inspired by high-density trading hubs. By aligning all your Markets along a single, straight thoroughfare (often referred to as a "Fruit Street" design), you create an incredibly efficient money-farming route.

### The Fortified Castle Core
For a grand medieval aesthetic, reserve your central land plot exclusively for castle structures. Encircle this district with high stone walls and defensive towers, keeping your residential houses and farming zones on the outer fields.

| Layout Concept | Recommended Elements | Pros | Cons |
|--- |--- |--- |--- |
| **Compact Village** | Houses, Markets, Basic Roads | Extremely cheap, fast setup, high early income | Hard to expand, looks crowded in late game |
| **Market Street** | Markets, Straight Roads, Trees | Highly efficient payout collection, neat look | Requires long, flat stretches of land |
| **Walled Castle** | Towers, Walls, Gates, Gardens | Beautiful fantasy aesthetic, clear boundaries | Very expensive, consumes massive building space |
| **Waterfront Quarter** | Water, Bridges, Houses, Paths | Highly realistic, unique natural layouts | Terrain manipulation can be tricky to align |

<Callout type="info" title="Offline Preparation">
Before logging off, ensure all your building slots are filled with active income-producing structures. Your kingdom will continue to generate passive gold while you are offline, allowing you to return to a massive budget for your next expansion phase.
</Callout>
